4-Axis CNC Hot-Wire Cutter

  • Strengths demonstrated: Programming and Debugging, Soldering, Mechanical Design and Assembly
  • Tools used: Solidworks, Aruino Compiler, Stepper Motors
  • School Team: University of Toronto HPVDT
  • Project date: 2021 Summer
  • Competition Information: The Kremer International Marathon Competition

The HPVDT team is competing for the Kremer International Marathon Prize, and I was tasked with developing a CNC machine to manufacture the wings from a 3D CAD model. I had never previously built a CNC machine, but I had a good understanding of how the machined worked and operated. I compartmentalized the tasks of the project, researched and delivered my findings. I was able to learn quite a bit about stepper motors, drivers, limit switches and arduino programming to deliver a workign CNC machine with instructions on how to use the software to create wings for the vehicle.

This CNC machine takes two 2D cross sections from a 3D CAD model and translates it into G-code to cut out a long prism with the two cross sections at each end. This machine was able to provide very accurate cuts that followed our 3D CAD perfectly. This was done using 4 axes controlled by a motor and leadscrew, two axes moved one end of a hot wire vertically and horizontally while the other two moved the other end of the hot wire.
